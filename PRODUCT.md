# Product

## Register

brand

## Users
Women aged 20–45 in Taboão da Serra and nearby neighborhoods of Greater São Paulo, booking nail appointments as a moment of weekly self-care. They arrive via Instagram DMs, Google search, and word-of-mouth. They use phones more than desktops. They want to feel like they are getting something special, not just a service.

## Product Purpose
Safira Nails is a boutique nail studio. The tagline "valiosa como uma safira, cuidada como uma joia rara" (precious as a sapphire, cared for like a rare jewel) sets the register: above average, but warm and approachable. The site converts visitors into WhatsApp bookings and drives product sales through the loja. Success is a first-time visitor who decides this studio is worth the appointment.

## Brand Personality
Artisanal, warm, aspirational. The neighborhood atelier that makes you feel like royalty.

## Anti-references
- Generic SaaS landing pages (gradient-blob hero, floating metric badges, purple-on-lavender)
- Corporate nail chain sites (impersonal, sterile)
- Over-the-top fashion luxury (cold, intimidating)
- Pinterest-core beauty aesthetics (pastel excess, infantile)
- Stock-photo tourism (looks like a template, not a real place)

## Design Principles
1. Craftsmanship shows in the details. Every element must look considered, not filled in.
2. Warmth before luxury. Aspirational but never cold; the studio is a neighborhood institution.
3. Photography carries the brand. Nail art is visual, the gallery does the selling.
4. Trust feels local. Social proof should feel human, not SaaS-inflated metrics.
5. WhatsApp is the primary conversion. Every surface leads there.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
WCAG AA minimum. Prioritize mobile legibility: primary users are on mid-range Android phones in daylight. Reduced motion preference honored via Framer Motion. Dark mode supported as a browsing preference, not as a core design register.
